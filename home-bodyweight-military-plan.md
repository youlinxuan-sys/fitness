## Reality Check

Dropping from 90 kg to 80 kg is realistic over roughly 3–5 months for a sustainable cut. In 1–3 months, the goal is visible change: smaller waist, better push-up/squat capacity, lower resting fatigue, improved photos, and initial weight drop.

Target pace:

- Weight loss: 0.5–1.0% bodyweight/week = about 0.45–0.9 kg/week at 90 kg.
- 12 weeks realistic target: about 82–85 kg if adherence is good; 80 kg may be possible but aggressive.
- Keep strength/reps improving while waist drops. If reps collapse, calorie deficit/recovery is too harsh.

## Core Rules

- Train 6 days/week, 15–30 min/day.
- One active recovery day/week.
- Every session: warm-up 3–5 min, main work 12–22 min, cooldown 2–3 min.
- RPE 7–9: hard but controlled. No sloppy reps.
- Military style = discipline, simple standards, progressive overload, not self-destruction.

## Daily Warm-up — 3–5 min

Use before every training day.

1. March/high knees in place — 45 sec
2. Arm circles + scapular push-up — 45 sec
3. Hip hinge good morning — 15 reps
4. Dead bug — 8/side
5. Reverse Nordic short range or quad rock-back — 8 reps
6. Squat-to-stand — 6 reps

## Week A

### A1 — Squat + Push Barracks

Main circuit, 18–24 min. Do 4–6 rounds.

- Tempo squat — 15 reps, 3 sec down
- Push-up — 8–20 reps
- Split squat — 10/side
- Pike push-up — 6–12 reps
- Dead bug — 10/side
- Rest 45–75 sec

Coach-menu mapping:

- Goblet/bar squat → tempo squat + split squat
- Seated barbell press / landmine press → pike push-up
- Close-grip bench → push-up variations

Progression:

- Week 1: 4 rounds
- Week 3: 5 rounds
- Week 5+: 6 rounds or harder push-up variation

### A2 — Pull Substitute + Core

No pull-up bar, so use back/posture/isometric work. Do 4–6 rounds.

- Prone Y-T-W raise — 8 each shape
- Reverse snow angel — 12 reps
- Superman pull-down — 12 reps
- Table/towel isometric row if safe — 20–30 sec
- Side plank — 20–40 sec/side
- Hollow hold — 15–30 sec
- Rest 45–75 sec

If a sturdy table is available, add table inverted row 6–12 reps. Only use if stable.

Coach-menu mapping:

- Pull-up / inverted row / seated row → table row or prone Y-T-W + isometric row
- High pull / clean technique → explosive posture and core control, not replicated without equipment
- Side plank stays

Progression:

- Add 5 sec to holds weekly.
- Add 1 round when all reps are clean.

### A3 — Hinge + Chest + Split-Leg Strength

Do 4–6 rounds.

- Single-leg Romanian deadlift, bodyweight — 10/side, slow
- Glute bridge — 20 reps
- Feet-elevated or regular push-up — 8–18 reps
- Reverse lunge — 10/side
- Plank shoulder tap — 20 total
- Rest 60–90 sec

Coach-menu mapping:

- Trap-bar deadlift → single-leg RDL + glute bridge
- Half-range bench → push-up
- Landmine split squat → reverse lunge
- Landmine shoulder press → plank shoulder tap / pike push-up optional

## Week B

Coach-menu mapping:

- Rack squat → pause squat
- Single-arm DB/KB shoulder press → push-up/pike push-up

Coach-menu mapping:

### B3 — Sumo/Hinge + Hamstring + Push

Do 4–6 rounds.

Coach-menu mapping:

- Sumo deadlift → sumo squat + hamstring walkout
- DB chest press → slow negative push-up

- Landmine shoulder press → walkout push-up / pike push-up optional

## Conditioning Days

### Conditioning 1 — 15–20 min EMOM

Every minute on the minute. Complete reps, rest for remaining time.

Cycle 4 exercises for 4–5 rounds:

1. Burpee — 6–10 reps
2. Squat — 20–25 reps
3. Push-up — 8–15 reps
4. Mountain climber — 30–40 sec

Start with 16 min. Build to 20 min.

### Conditioning 2 — Military Density Circuit

AMRAP 15–25 min: as many clean rounds as possible.

- Jumping jack — 40
- Push-up — 10
- Reverse lunge — 10/side
- Plank — 30 sec
- Squat thrust or burpee — 8
- Hollow hold — 20 sec

Record rounds. Beat last week by 0.5–1 round or cleaner reps.

## Saturday — Ruck / Zone 2

Choose one:

- Brisk walk 30–45 min
- Easy jog/walk intervals 25–35 min
- Backpack ruck 25–40 min, only if allowed later; start 5% bodyweight, max 10% early on

Intensity: can speak short sentences, cannot sing.

Heart-rate target for age 33–34, resting HR 60–66:

- Estimated HRmax: about 184–187 bpm; use 185 bpm for planning.
- Heart-rate reserve (HRR): about 119–125 bpm.
- Zone 2 by HRR 60–70%: about 135–150 bpm.
- Easier aerobic base / long brisk walk: about 125–140 bpm.
- Harder walk-jog upper cap for most sessions: about 150 bpm.
- Formula: Target HR = Resting HR + intensity × (HRmax - Resting HR).
- Practical rule: most brisk walks should stay around 130–145 bpm; 145–150 is okay briefly if still conversational. If breathing gets too hard to speak short sentences, slow down.

## 12-Week Progression

### Weeks 1–2 — Base Camp

- 15–20 min sessions
- 4 rounds on strength days
- EMOM 12–16 min
- Learn form and baseline

### Weeks 3–6 — Build Phase

- 20–25 min sessions
- 5 rounds on strength days
- Reduce rest by 10–15 sec
- Add tempo: slow eccentrics, pauses

### Weeks 7–10 — Assault Phase

- 25–30 min sessions
- 5–6 rounds
- Harder variations: decline push-up, jump squat, longer side plank, more burpees
- Saturday zone-2 becomes 35–45 min if recovery is good

### Weeks 11–12 — Test + Consolidate

Weekly tests:

- 2-min push-up max
- 2-min squat max
- Max plank
- 12-min run/walk distance or 1.5 km time
- Waist, weight, front/side/back photos

## Bodyweight Standards to Track

Record every Monday morning:

- Bodyweight
- Waist at navel
- Resting energy/soreness 1–5
- Push-up 2-min test every 4 weeks
- Squat 2-min test every 4 weeks
- Plank max every 4 weeks

## Nutrition Guardrails

Training alone will not reliably move 90 kg to 80 kg. Use these minimum rules:

- Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg target body weight = about 130–175 g/day.
- Calorie deficit: start around -300 to -500 kcal/day.
- Aim for 0.45–0.9 kg/week weight loss.
- If losing >1 kg/week and workouts crash, eat slightly more.
- Build meals around protein + vegetables + controlled carbs.
- Cut liquid calories, alcohol, late snacks first.
- Sleep target: 7+ hours.

## Safety Notes

- Stop for sharp pain, chest pain, dizziness, unusual shortness of breath, or joint pain.
- Soreness is acceptable; joint pain is not.
- Military style should make training automatic, not reckless.
